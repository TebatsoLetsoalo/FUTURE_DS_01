## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing business sales performance using transactional sales data. The objective is to identify key trends and insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

The analysis examines:

1. Which regions generate the highest sales and profit.
2. Which products contribute most to overall revenue.
3. Seasonal trends and patterns in sales over time.
4. Underperforming regions that may require improvement.

By leveraging data analytics and visualization tools, this project aims to provide meaningful insights into business performance and highlight opportunities for growth, efficiency, and improved profitability.

## Data Cleaning

The dataset was cleaned to ensure accuracy, consistency, and suitability for analysis.
- Checked for duplicate rows to avoid double counting
- Checked for missing values by reviewing incomplete records
- Standardized date format to yyyy-mm-dd - Checked if text columns (Region, Category, Product) are clean
- Checked for extra spaces and standardized capitalization
- Checked for numeric columns stored as text into numbers
- Checked for negative and unrealistic values 
- Calculated Profit_Margin to evaluate profitability
- Month: A Month column was extracted from the Order Date to enable monthly trend analysis and time-based visualizations.
- Year: A Year column was created to support yearly comparisons and filtering in the dashboard.
- Profit Margin: A Profit Margin column was calculated to evaluate the efficiency and profitability of products and regions. This metric helps identify how much profit is generated per unit of sales and is essential for financial performance analysis.

These additional features improved the ability to analyze trends, compare performance, and generate meaningful business insights.

## Key insights

1. Sales Trend

Sales exhibit noticeable peaks in March, September, and November, indicating periods of higher demand. This suggests that the business performs strongest during specific times of the year, possibly due to seasonal demand or promotional activities.

2. Product Performance

The Canon imageCLASS copier generates the highest sales and significantly outperforms other products. This indicates a strong reliance on a single high-performing product, highlighting its importance to overall revenue.

3. Regional Performance

The West region generates the highest profit, followed by the East, while the Central region contributes the least. This indicates uneven regional performance and suggests that some regions are more efficient or better optimized than others.

4. Profitability

The average profit margin of 12.03% indicates that the business retains approximately $0.12 for every $1 of sales. This reflects moderate profitability, with potential room for improvement.

5. Overall Observation

While sales performance is strong, profitability varies across regions and products. This suggests that high sales do not always translate into high profit, indicating possible cost or pricing inefficiencies.

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

-Leverage high-performing products: Promote and expand sales of top-performing products like the Canon imageCLASS copier.

-Improve underperforming regions: Investigate and optimize operations in low-profit regions such as Central.

-Enhance profitability: Review pricing strategies and cost structures to improve profit margins.

-Capitalize on peak periods: Focus marketing and inventory efforts during high-demand months (March, September, November).

-Diversify product performance: Reduce reliance on a single product by strengthening the performance of other products.

## Conclusions
The analysis highlights key areas of strength, such as strong sales trends and top-performing products, while also identifying opportunities to improve profitability and regional performance. By implementing targeted strategies, the business can enhance both revenue and efficiency.
